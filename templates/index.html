About GliomaScope

Help, documentation, and information

About the Author

Empowering you to explore and understand at the genomic level within Bioinformatics.

My name is Mishael Beppin, Student ID: 2732522 and I'm an MSc Genomic Medicine student with a keen interest in bioinformatics and cancer research. During my studies, I saw how complex and time-consuming it can be to work with large transcriptomic datasets, especially for researchers without a programming background. I created GliomaScope to make this process easier, providing an accessible platform for exploring glioma transcriptomes, uncovering patterns, and generating insights that could support both research and clinical understanding. This project combines my technical skills with my passion for turning genomic data into knowledge that can make a difference.

User Guide
Getting Started
  1. Data Upload: Use options 1-2 to load your data (GEO datasets or custom files)
  2. Data Exploration: Option 3 to explore and filter your dataset
  3. Visualization: Options 4-6 for geographic and dimensionality reduction plots
  4. Analysis: Options 7-10 for gene expression analysis and visualization
Function Overview
1. GEO Dataset Download: Download and load datasets from NCBI GEO database
2. Upload Custom Data: Upload and format your own CSV/TSV files
3. Data Exploration: Explore, filter, and preview your data
4. Geographic Visualization: Create maps showing study locations
5. PCA Analysis: Principal Component Analysis for dimensionality reduction
6. UMAP Analysis: UMAP for non-linear dimensionality reduction
7. Differential Expression: Compare gene expression between groups
8. Gene Exploration: Explore individual gene expression patterns
9. Chromosome Mapping: Map genes to chromosomal locations
10. Heatmap Visualization: Generate expression heatmaps for gene sets
